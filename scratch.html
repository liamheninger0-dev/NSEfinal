Who We Are

Beyond the Track

New Standard Elite isn't just a running group — it's a movement. Built at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah, NSE was created to push the boundaries of what collegiate athletes can accomplish when they combine elite training with genuine community.

The club is anchored by three of BYU's most competitive distance runners: Tayvon Kitchen, Isaac Hedengren, and Carter Cutting. Together they document their journey, share training insights, and inspire a new generation of runners.

The name says everything: we do not meet the standard. We set it.

The Blueprint

How NSE Trains

A typical NSE training week blends intensity, recovery, and intentionality. Here's the weekly training structure:

```
  1. Monday — Speed Development
    • Warm-up: 2 mile easy jog + drills
    • Main: 8 × 400m at race pace with 90s rest
    • Cool-down: 1 mile easy + stretching
  2. Tuesday — Aerobic Base
    • 6–10 mile easy long run
    • Strides on the track (4 × 100m)
    • Foam rolling & mobility work
  3. Wednesday — Threshold Run
    • 3 × 2 miles at tempo effort
    • 2 min rest between sets
    • Focus: comfortably uncomfortable pace
  4. Thursday — Recovery + Strength
    • Easy 5 miles
    • Gym: core, glutes, hip stability
    • Optional: pool or bike cross-training
  5. Friday — Race Simulation
    • Warm-up + race-specific workout
    • E.g., 3 × 800m at 5K goal pace
    • Mental prep & visualization
  6. Saturday — Long Run
    • 12–16 miles for distance runners
    • 8–10 miles for 800m/1500m specialists
    • Progressive effort through the run
  7. Sunday — Full Rest
    • No structured training
    • Light walk or yoga optional
    • Focus: sleep, nutrition, reflection
```
